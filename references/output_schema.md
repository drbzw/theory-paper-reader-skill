# Required Output Schema

## 1. `01_theory_tutorial.md`

Required numbered sections:

1. What problem the paper is actually solving
2. The friction-free benchmark
3. Agents, timing, and information
4. The central economic tension
5. How the model works
6. Main results and what is non-trivial
7. Comparative statics
8. Welfare and distribution
9. What an empirical researcher should take away
10. Limits and strongest likely objection

## 2. `02_structural_reconstruction.md`

Required sections:

1. Paper and source metadata
2. Paper type
3. Agents
4. Timing and commitment
5. Information structure
6. Preferences and payoffs
7. Actions and strategies
8. State variables and outcomes
9. Constraints
10. Equilibrium or solution concept
11. First-best and second-best
12. Main propositions and proof status
13. Comparative statics
14. Welfare implications
15. Load-bearing assumptions
16. Boundary conditions and unresolved items

Every substantive entry must include an epistemic label and source locator.

## 3. `03_family_classification.md`

Required sections:

1. Classification verdict and web-verification status
2. Primary family and specific subfamily
3. Secondary or extension families
4. Candidate families considered
5. Comparative adjudication
6. Closest canonical ancestor
7. Theory lineage: inherited, changed, and new
8. Relabeling assessment
9. Irreducibility condition
10. Classification confidence
11. External evidence log
12. Remaining uncertainty

## 5. `05_library_update_proposal.md`

Required sections:

1. Recommendation
2. Rationale
3. Proposed library location
4. What should be added
5. What should not be added
6. Duplication and relabeling risk
7. Human review questions
8. Mutation control

State explicitly that no library file was modified and that any update requires separate human approval.

## 6. `06_audit_report.md`

Generated by `scripts/validate_outputs.py`. It must state PASS, WARNING, or FAIL and clarify that deterministic validation does not certify the substantive economics.
