# Web Verification Rules

## 1. Verification is mandatory for final lineage claims

Model knowledge may generate candidate families and likely ancestors, but it is not sufficient evidence for a final classification. The final report must state either:

- `VERIFIED` — the relevant lineage claims were checked against external sources; or
- `PROVISIONAL — WEB VERIFICATION PENDING` — web access was unavailable or evidence remained insufficient.

## 2. Source priority

Prefer sources in this order:

1. the published paper or official publisher page;
2. author-hosted or institutional working-paper versions;
3. NBER, CEPR, SSRN, DOI, or recognized institutional repositories;
4. author slides, seminar materials, or course notes that directly describe the model;
5. the canonical ancestor paper itself;
6. authoritative textbooks or survey articles for family-level background.

Do not treat search-result snippets, unsourced summaries, or generated text as verification.

## 3. Search sequence

First search the focal paper itself. Inspect phrases such as “we follow,” “building on,” “in the spirit of,” “our model extends,” and “the closest paper.” Then search the model section's key citations and candidate ancestors.

For each proposed ancestor, compare:

- agents and roles;
- timing;
- information structure;
- contractibility and commitment;
- constraints;
- equilibrium concept;
- main mechanism and result.

A paper's presence in the bibliography is not enough.

## 4. Adversarial verification

Search for evidence that could overturn the initial classification. If the model appears to be signaling, check whether the uninformed party actually designs a menu first. If it appears to be incomplete contracts, check whether residual control rights and non-contractible investment are truly load-bearing.

## 5. Citation hygiene

Record the title, authors, year, source type, and the specific point supported. Exclude unverified titles from definitive statements. If bibliographic details conflict across versions, report the conflict.

## 6. No-web behavior

Without web access, complete the tutorial and structural reconstruction, generate candidate families, and produce a provisional card. Do not claim that the closest ancestor or intellectual lineage has been verified.
